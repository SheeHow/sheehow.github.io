## About Me  
I am Shihao Li, a second-year Ph.D. student at **The University of Texas at Austin**, researching **Proton Exchange Membrane Fuel Cells (PEMFCs)** for heavy-duty applications. As a graduate research assistant in the **Advanced Power Systems and Control Lab**, advised by [**Dr. Dongmei "Maggie" Chen**](https://www.me.utexas.edu/people/faculty-directory/chen), I develop **anisotropic Membrane Electrode Assemblies (MEAs)** to enhance fuel cell **durability, efficiency, and scalability**. My work integrates **advanced modeling, robust control strategies, and data-driven diagnostics** to improve long-term fuel cell performance under real-world conditions.  

## Research Interests  
I focus on **fuel cell degradation mechanisms, predictive modeling, and system optimization** to enhance efficiency and lifespan. My research spans **multiscale modeling, electrochemical analysis, and control strategies** to ensure stable operation under dynamic duty cycles. Additionally, I apply **data-driven approaches** for real-time diagnostics, predictive maintenance, and system-level optimization.  

## Current Work  
My current projects involve **scalable MEA designs, real-time degradation modeling, and fuel cell duty cycle analysis**. Through computational modeling and experimental validation, I aim to advance **sustainable, commercially viable hydrogen fuel cell technologies** for heavy-duty transportation and industrial applications.
